Log 1: Art Instructor
I volunteered as an art instructor at my former elementary school, where I designed and led creative art lessons for young students. My role involved developing engaging lesson plans, introducing foundational art concepts, and guiding children through various artistic techniques and projects. Through this experience, I aimed to foster creativity, encourage self-expression, and build confidence in the students. It was a rewarding opportunity to give back to the school community that shaped my own early education, while also helping to inspire a new generation of young artists.
Difficulty: Medium Duration: 1 Year Reward: +30 Patience
Log 2: Ernie Banks Statue
As a contributing sculptor for the Ernie Banks statue project at Booker T. Washington HSPVA, I had the unique opportunity to sculpt the shoes of the statue. This work was part of a larger collaborative effort to honor the legendary Chicago Cubs player and celebrate his enduring legacy in sports and culture. The experience taught me the value of teamwork in a creative context and reinforced my belief in the power of public art to foster community spirit and preserve cultural heritage. By sculpting part of this iconic statue, I was able to give back to the community through art and contribute to a piece that stands as a testament to perseverance, hope, and excellence.
Difficulty: Hard Duration: 5 Weeks Reward: +40 Collaboration
Log 3: Operation Kindness Charity
I donated original dog drawings to support a fundraising initiative for Operation Kindness, a leading no-kill animal shelter. The artwork was created specifically to help raise awareness and funds for the organization's mission of providing care and finding homes for homeless pets. By contributing my artistic skills to this cause, I aimed to engage the community and encourage donations through visually appealing and heartfelt illustrations. This experience allowed me to leverage my creativity in a meaningful way, supporting a cause I am passionate about and contributing to the welfare of animals in need.
Difficulty: Easy to Medium Duration: 3 Weeks Reward: +30 Community Impact
Log 4: Arty for The Planet
I contributed two large-scale chalk pieces to the Arty for the Planet project at the Lacerte Family Zoo, a creative initiative that raised awareness about environmental conservation through art. These pieces were designed to engage viewers, featuring vibrant depictions of endangered species and their habitats. By using art as a medium to convey a powerful message, I aimed to inspire a deeper connection between people and the natural world, bringing more attention to the need for conservation efforts. This experience allowed me to use my artistic skills to contribute to a cause I am passionate about and to bring visibility to environmental issues in a dynamic and accessible way.
Difficulty: Easy Duration: 1 Day Reward: +10 Volunteer
